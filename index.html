Types of wind farms

Onshore wind farms

They are currently the most common. They are located on land no less than 3 kilometers from the coast and feed on terrestrial air currents. The advantage that this location offers is its easy accessibility and proximity to the electric grid.

Learn More

Nearshore wind farms

They are also located on land, but less than 3 kilometers from the coast. The advantage of opting for this location is that it can harness both terrestrial winds and sea winds to produce energy.

Learn More

Offshore wind farms

These structures are built in the open sea several miles from the coast. Among their main benefits compared to land installations is that the wind's force is greater, at a lower altitude, and more regular than on land.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is wind energy and how do wind turbines work?

Wind energy (or wind power) refers to the process by which wind turbines convert the movement of wind into electricity. Wind is caused by the Sun’s uneven heating of the atmosphere, the irregularities of the Earth's surface, and the rotation of the Earth.

Does wind power help in the fight against climate change?

Yes. Unlike gas or coal-fired power stations, wind farms don't emit greenhouse gases: in 2019 alone, wind energy production prevented 198 million tons of CO₂ from entering the atmosphere; it also reduced the consumption of water that's often necessary to enable fossil-fuel power stations to operate.

What benefits do wind farms bring to the local area?

Wind farms bring immediate benefits to the local area and its economy. A good example is employment: jobs are created through spin-off activities that are generated and guaranteed by each of the project phases, from the initial development, to construction, right through to the maintenance of the facilities.

How big is a wind turbine?

The average size of onshore turbines being manufactured today is around 2.5-3 MW, with blades of about 50 metres length. It can power more than 1,500 average EU households.

How is a wind farm designed?

There are many factors at play when designing a wind farm. Ideally, the area should be as wide and open as possible in the prevailing wind direction, with few obstacles. Its visual influence needs to be considered – few, larger turbines are usually better than many smaller ones.

How efficient are wind turbines?

Wind turbines start operating at wind speeds of 4 to 5 metres per second and reach maximum power output at around 15 metres/second. At very high wind speeds, that is gale force winds of 25 metres/second, wind turbines shut down. A modern wind turbine produces electricity 70-85% of the time, but it generates different outputs depending on the wind speed.

Benefits of Wind Power

In addition, wind energy is a “native” energy, because it is available practically everywhere on the plant, which contributes to reducing energy imports and to creating wealth and local employment. For these reasons, producing electricity through wind energy and its efficient use contributes to sustainable development.
